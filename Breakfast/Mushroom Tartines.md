# Mushroom Tartines

[From Here](https://smittenkitchen.com/2017/03/mushroom-tartines/)

> Modeled on a croque monsieur, these open-faced tartines feature a Dijon-rich bechamel, piles of sautéed mushrooms, and melted cheese on hearty sourdough.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Unsalted Butter     | 2      | Tbsp  | (30 grams)                                           |
| All-purpose Flour   | 2      | Tbsp  | (15 grams)                                           |
| Milk                | 3/4    | cup   | (175 ml) Ideally whole, lowfat works                 |
| Nutmeg              |        |       | A few fresh gratings                                 |
| Salt & Pepper       |        |       | To taste                                             |
| Dijon Mustard       | 1      | Tbsp  | (15 grams) Smooth                                    |
||||
| Fresh Mushrooms     | 1 1/2  | lbs   | (680 grams) Cremini, white, or wild mix, thinly sliced|
| Olive Oil & Butter  |        |       | As needed for sautéing                               |
| Fresh Herbs         | 2      | tsp   | Minced mixed (rosemary, thyme, sage)                 |
| Salt & Pepper       |        |       | To taste                                             |
||||
| Sourdough Bread     | 1      | loaf  | Hearty white or whole wheat, 1lb, 3/4-inch slices    |
| Gruyere or Comte    | 8      | oz    | (225 grams) Coarsely grated                          |
| Flat-leaf Parsley   |        |       | Chopped, to garnish                                  |

## Instructions

1.  **Make Sauce:** In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat and stir in flour until a paste forms. Very slowly drizzle in milk, whisking constantly. Add nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring, until thickened (a couple of minutes). Scrape into a bowl, stir in Dijon, adjust seasonings, and set aside.
2.  **Prep Oven:** Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line largest baking sheet with foil.
3.  **Cook Mushrooms:** Wipe out skillet and heat over medium-high. Add a glug of olive oil (or mix with butter). When hot, sear mushrooms in batches (1/3 to 1/2 at a time) with some herbs. Brown for 2-3 minutes without stirring, then stir and cook until tender and liquid evaporates (about 5 minutes). Season with salt and pepper. Repeat.
4.  **Assemble:** Spread bread in one layer on baking sheet. Schmear each slice to the edges with sauce (thin coat). Heap with mushrooms (use them all). Sprinkle cheese over, pressing it in if necessary.
5.  **Bake:** Bake for 10 minutes until cheese is melted. Transfer to broiler and cook until tops are browned (a few minutes more, watch closely).
6.  **Serve:** Scatter with parsley and serve hot.

**Notes:**

* **Bread:** If bread is soft, lightly pre-toast it. If sturdy/crusty, no need.
* **Sauce:** Recipe makes 3/4 cup bechamel (approx 1 Tbsp per slice). Scale up if desired: 3 Tbsp each butter/flour, 1 cup + 2 Tbsp milk, heaped Tbsp Dijon.
* **Serving:** Serves 4 heartily or 8 with sides. Excellent with a big green salad.
