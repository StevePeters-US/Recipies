# Mushroom Tartines

[From Here](https://smittenkitchen.com/2017/03/mushroom-tartines/)

> Open-faced mushroom sandwiches inspired by a croque monsieur, featuring a Dijon-rich bechamel, sautéed herbs, and melted Gruyère.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Unsalted butter     | 2      | Tbsp  | For bechamel                                         |
| All-purpose flour   | 2      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Milk                | 3/4    | cup   | Whole milk recommended                               |
| Nutmeg              |        |       | Freshly grated                                       |
| Salt                |        |       | To taste                                             |
| Black pepper        |        |       | To taste                                             |
| Dijon mustard       | 1      | Tbsp  | Smooth                                               |
||||
| Fresh mushrooms     | 1 1/2  | lbs   | Cremini, white, or wild; thinly sliced               |
| Olive oil           |        |       | For sautéing                                         |
| Unsalted butter     |        |       | For sautéing                                         |
| Fresh herbs         | 2      | tsp   | Minced rosemary, thyme, and/or sage                  |
||||
| Sourdough bread     | 1      | loaf  | Hearty, sliced 3/4-inch thick                        |
| Gruyère cheese      | 8      | oz    | Coarsely grated (or Comte)                           |
| Fresh parsley       |        |       | Chopped, for garnish                                 |

## Instructions

1. **Make Bechamel:** Melt 2 tbsp butter in a small saucepan over medium-low heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1 minute without browning. Slowly whisk in the milk and bring to a simmer. Cook until thickened, about 3 to 4 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the nutmeg, salt, pepper, and Dijon mustard.
2. **Sauté Mushrooms:** Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat with a glug of olive oil and a bit of butter. Sauté the mushrooms in batches until browned and any liquid has evaporated, about 5 minutes. Stir in the fresh herbs and season with salt and pepper.
3. **Prepare Oven:** Heat oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with foil.
4. **Assemble:** Place bread slices on the baking sheet. Spread a thin layer of bechamel on each slice, reaching all the way to the edges. Heap the mushrooms onto the slices and press the grated cheese into the top.
5. **Bake:** Bake for 10 minutes until the cheese is melted. Transfer to the broiler for 1-2 minutes until the tops are browned and bubbly.
6. **Serve:** Garnish with fresh parsley. Serve immediately with a knife and fork.

**Notes:**

* **Bread:** If using softer bread, lightly pre-toast the slices before adding the bechamel.
* **Make-Ahead:** The bechamel and mushrooms can be prepared in advance and stored in the fridge. Reheat slightly before assembling.
