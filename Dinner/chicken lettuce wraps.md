# Chicken Lettuce Wraps

[From Here](https://www.thekitchn.com/how-to-make-chicken-lettuce-wraps-cooking-lessons-from-the-kitchn-220124)

> Chicken lettuce wraps are super easy, and make a surprisingly quick weeknight meal. It hits that magical middle zone of being both very satisfying and also surprisingly light and refreshing.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Hoisin Sauce        | 3 - 5  | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Soy Sauce           | 2      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Rice Vinegar        | 2      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Toasted Sesame Oil  | 1      | tsp   |                                                      |
| Cornstarch          | 1      | tsp   | Optional, for thicker sauce                          |
||||
| Vegetable Oil       | 2      | tsp   | Divided                                              |
| Ground Chicken      | 1      | lb    |                                                      |
| Mushrooms           | 8      | oz    | White button or cremini, finely chopped              |
| Water Chestnuts     | 1      | can   | (8 oz) drained and finely chopped                    |
| Garlic              | 3      | cloves| Minced                                               |
| Fresh Ginger        | 1      | Tbsp  | Peeled and minced                                    |
| Scallions           | 1/2    | cup   | Thinly sliced (from about 6 scallions), divided      |
||||
| Bibb Lettuce        | 2      | heads | Or butter lettuce                                    |
| Red Pepper Flakes   |        |       | Optional for serving                                 |
| Sriracha            |        |       | Optional for serving                                 |

## Instructions

1.  **Make Sauce:** Place 3 tablespoons of the hoisin sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and toasted sesame oil in a small bowl and whisk. If you'd like a more thickened, glossy sauce, whisk in cornstarch (optional); place near the stove.
2.  **Cook Chicken:** Heat 1 teaspoon of the oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add ground chicken and cook, breaking it up into small pieces, until cooked through and no longer pink, 7 to 8 minutes. Transfer the cooked chicken to a clean bowl; set aside.
3.  **Cook Veggies:** Add the remaining 1 teaspoon oil to the same pan. Add finely chopped mushrooms (and any optional vegetables like diced onions or peppers), and cook, stirring occasionally until tender, 4 to 5 minutes.
4.  **Add Aromatics:** Stir in finely chopped water chestnuts, minced garlic, and minced fresh ginger. Cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
5.  **Combine:** Return the chicken to the pan and add 1/2 of the scallions.
6.  **Sauce & Simmer:** Add the sauce and cook, stirring occasionally, until bubbling and the sauce is warmed through, 30 to 60 seconds. Taste and add more hoisin sauce if desired.
7.  **Serve:** Serve hot mixture in lettuce leaves. Top with remaining scallions, sriracha, or red pepper flakes.

**Notes:**

* **Make Ahead:** The cooked chicken filling keeps very well in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat just what you need and add a splash of chicken broth if the mixture seems dry.
* **Swaps:** You can use ground turkey instead of chicken. For gluten-free, use GF hoisin and tamari.
* **Veggies:** You can add other finely diced vegetables like onions, bell peppers, or carrots with the mushrooms.
