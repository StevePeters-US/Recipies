# Soft Mahi Mahi Tacos with Ginger-Lime Dressing

[From Here](https://www.allrecipes.com/recipe/164313/soft-mahi-mahi-tacos-with-ginger-lime-dressing/)

> A warm, flavorful dish filled with mahi-mahi, fresh fruit and ginger-lime dressing. Inspired by fresh seafood recipes in Mexico and Central America.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Olive Oil           | 1      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Mahi Mahi Fillets   | 6      |       | (3 oz each)                                          |
| Salt & Pepper       |        |       | To taste                                             |
||||
| Sour Cream          | 1/3    | cup   |                                                      |
| Lime Juice          | 1      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Ginger Root         | 1      | tsp   | Minced, fresh                                        |
| Ground Cumin        | 1/4    | tsp   |                                                      |
| Cayenne Pepper      | 1      | dash  |                                                      |
||||
| Mango               | 1      | large | Peeled, seeded and diced                             |
| Fresh Pineapple     | 1      | cup   | Diced                                                |
| Avocado             | 1      |       | Peeled, pitted and diced                             |
| Jalapeno Pepper     | 1      |       | Minced                                               |
||||
| Flour Tortillas     | 6      |       | (6 inch) Warmed                                      |
| Fresh Cilantro      | 1      | cup   | Chopped                                              |

## Instructions

1.  **Cook Fish:** Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season the mahi-mahi with salt and pepper. Cook the fillets in the hot oil until the fish is golden brown on each side, and no longer translucent in the center, about 3 minutes per side.
2.  **Make Sauce:** Meanwhile, whisk together the sour cream, lime juice, ginger, cumin, cayenne pepper, salt and pepper to taste; set aside.
3.  **Make Salsa:** Gently combine the mango, pineapple, avocado, and jalapeno in a bowl.
4.  **Assemble:** Place a cooked mahi-mahi fillet into the center of a warmed tortilla. Place a scoop of the mango salsa onto the fish, then drizzle with the sour cream sauce, and finish with a generous pinch of chopped cilantro.

**Notes:**

* **Serving:** Serve with chips and salsa on the side.
* **Fish:** If you cannot find Mahi Mahi, other firm white fish like cod or halibut can be substituted.
