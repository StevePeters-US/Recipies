# Mushroom Quinoa Risotto (Quinotto)

[From Here](https://www.notenoughcinnamon.com/mushroom-quinoa-risotto-quinotto/)

> A twist on the classic risotto, this quinotto recipe stars quinoa instead of rice to make a fabulous Italian-inspired dish. Between the light nutty taste of the quinoa, the earthy flavor of the mushrooms and the lemon zing, you will love this easy and healthy meatless meal.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Mixed Mushrooms     | 3      | cups  | Sliced (Swiss brown, oyster, shiitake, black funghi) |
| Olive Oil           | 1      | Tbsp  |                                                      |
| Quinoa              | 2      | cups  | Dry                                                  |
| Butter              | 1      | Tbsp  | Or refined coconut oil                               |
| Garlic              | 4      | cloves| Minced                                               |
| Brown Onion         | 1      |       | Finely sliced                                        |
| Chicken Stock       | 4 1/2  | cups  | Or vegetable stock for vegetarian                    |
| Chives              | 1/2    | bunch | Finely chopped (save some for serving)               |
| Lemon               | 1      |       | Juice and zest                                       |
| Fresh Thyme         | 2      | tsp   |                                                      |
| White Wine Vinegar  | 1/3    | cup   |                                                      |
| Soy Sauce           | 1      | Tbsp  | Or tamari (for gluten free)                          |
| Parmesan Cheese     | 3/4    | cup   | Grated (or nutritional yeast)                        |
| Salt & Pepper       |        |       | To taste                                             |

## Instructions

1.  **Sauté Mushrooms:** In a medium skillet, sauté the sliced mushrooms with a little bit of olive oil until they are cooked but still a bit firm. Remove from the heat and set aside.
2.  **Toast Quinoa:** In a large pot on medium-high heat, toast the quinoa in butter (or oil, if using), stirring regularly, until fragrant.
3.  **Aromatics:** Add minced garlic and sliced onion and sauté until softened.
4.  **Simmer:** Turn down the heat and cup by cup, add stock, stirring regularly.
5.  **Combine:** Once all the liquid has been reduced, add the rest of the ingredients: reserved mushrooms, chives, lemon juice and zest, thyme, vinegar, soy sauce (or tamari) and Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast). Stir well for a few minutes, and then remove from the heat.
6.  **Serve:** Serve immediately with extra chives and parmesan.

**Notes:**

* **Gluten Free:** Use tamari instead of soy sauce and make sure the stock is gluten free.
* **Vegan/Dairy Free:** Use coconut oil instead of butter, vegetable stock instead of chicken, and nutritional yeast instead of parmesan.
