# Oatmeal Cookies

[From Here](https://sugarspunrun.com/big-soft-oatmeal-cookies/)

> These soft, chewy, bakery-style oatmeal cookies are a classic favorite with a perfect balance of sweetness and hearty texture.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Unsalted butter     | 1      | cup   | Softened (226g)                                      |
| Light brown sugar   | 1      | cup   | Firmly packed (200g)                                 |
| Granulated sugar    | 1/2    | cup   | (100g)                                               |
| Eggs                | 2      | large | Room temperature preferred                           |
| Vanilla extract     | 1 1/2  | tsp   |                                                      |
||||
| All-purpose flour   | 1 3/4  | cup   | (215g)                                               |
| Cornstarch          | 2      | tsp   |                                                      |
| Baking soda         | 1      | tsp   |                                                      |
| Table salt          | 3/4    | tsp   |                                                      |
| Ground cinnamon     | 3/4    | tsp   |                                                      |
| Old-fashioned oats  | 3      | cups  | Rolled oats (285g)                                   |
||||
| Optional add-ins    | 1 1/2  | cups  | Chocolate chips, chopped nuts, or raisins            |

## Instructions

1. **Cream Butter:** Beat softened butter in the bowl of a stand mixer (or using an electric beater) for about 30 seconds.
2. **Add Sugars:** Add light brown sugar and granulated sugar. Beat until light and fluffy, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.
3. **Add Eggs:** Add eggs one at a time, beating until combined.
4. **Flavor:** Stir in the vanilla extract.
5. **Dry Ingredients:** In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
6. **Combine:** Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter mixture until completely combined.
7. **Fold in Oats:** Gradually stir in the oats (and any optional raisins, chips, or nuts) until well incorporated.
8. **Chill:** Cover the dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to 3 days).
9. **Prepare:** Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) and line cookie sheets with parchment paper.
10. **Scoop:** Drop dough by rounded 2-tablespoon-sized balls onto the parchment paper, spacing them at least 2 inches apart. For uniform cookies, roll the dough into smooth balls.
11. **Bake:** Bake for 10-12 minutes. The edges should be slightly browned while the centers still look slightly underbaked.
12. **Cool:** Allow cookies to cool completely on the cookie sheet before serving.

**Notes:**

* **Cornstarch Substitute:** If you don't have cornstarch, you can substitute an equal amount of flour. The cookies will be slightly flatter and less soft.
* **Quick Oats:** Quick oats can be used as a substitute for old-fashioned oats, but the cookies will be less chewy and hearty.
* **Underbake Slightly:** Removing the cookies when the centers still look a bit soft ensures they stay chewy for days.
