# Oatmeal Cookies

[From Here](https://sugarspunrun.com/big-soft-oatmeal-cookies/)

> These oatmeal cookies are soft, chewy, and bakery-style. An old-fashioned classic with perfectly balanced texture and sweetness.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient          | Qty    | Unit  | Notes                                                |
|:--------------------|:------:|:-----:|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| Unsalted Butter     | 1      | cup   | Softened (226 g)                                     |
| Light Brown Sugar   | 1      | cup   | Firmly packed (200 g)                                |
| Granulated Sugar    | 1/2    | cup   | (100 g)                                              |
| Eggs                | 2      | large | Room temperature preferred                           |
| Vanilla Extract     | 1 1/2  | tsp   |                                                      |
||||
| All-purpose Flour   | 1 3/4  | cup   | (215 g)                                              |
| Cornstarch          | 2      | tsp   |                                                      |
| Baking Soda         | 1      | tsp   |                                                      |
| Table Salt          | 3/4    | tsp   |                                                      |
| Ground Cinnamon     | 3/4    | tsp   |                                                      |
||||
| Rolled Oats         | 3      | cups  | Old-fashioned (285 g)                                |
| Mix-ins             | 1 1/2  | cups  | Chocolate chips, chopped nuts, or raisins (Optional) |

## Instructions

1.  **Cream Butter:** Beat butter in the bowl of a stand mixer (or using an electric beater) for about 30 seconds.
2.  **Add Sugars:** Add sugars and beat until light and fluffy (scrape down sides and bottom of bowl if needed).
3.  **Add Wet Ingredients:** Add eggs, one at a time, beating until combined. Stir in vanilla extract.
4.  **Mix Dry Ingredients:** In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cornstarch, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
5.  **Combine:** Gradually add flour mixture to butter mixture until completely combined. Scrape the sides and bottom of the bowl so ingredients are well-mixed.
6.  **Add Oats:** Gradually stir in oats until completely combined. If using raisins or chocolate chips, stir them in at this point.
7.  **Chill:** Cover dough with plastic wrap and place in the refrigerator to chill for at least 30 minutes (up to 3 days).
8.  **Prep Oven:** Preheat oven to 375F (190C) and line cookie sheets with parchment paper.
9.  **Scoop:** Drop cookie dough by rounded 2 Tablespoon-sized balls onto parchment paper, spacing at least 2" apart. Roll between palms for a smooth ball if desired.
10. **Bake:** Bake at 375F (190C) for 10-12 minutes. Edges should be slightly browned, centers may still look slightly underbaked.
11. **Cool:** Allow cookies to cool completely on the cookie sheet.

**Notes:**

* **Brown Sugar:** You may substitute dark brown sugar for an even softer and chewier cookie.
* **Oats:** You may substitute an equal amount of instant oats for the old-fashioned.
* **Storing:** Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days.
